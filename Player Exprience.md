The default experience should be responsive to the status of the league events. Some page analytics could help us understand what people are most interested in without guessing.
### When no league night is active
If no league night is active there are a few pages that make sense by default.
- **The League Stats & Records page** — This is a reasonable place to start. It promotes competition
- **The Forum** — Starting here promotes the social side of the league; potentially driving up engagement when nothing else is happening. This could be a good way to keep the league active between seasons. Could also be a place where people can organize for playing full courses.
- **My Stats** — This could be what people are most interested in.
- **The results of the previous event** — This let's people easily see how the last event went up until the next event is live.
### When an event is live but player cards HAVE NOT been published yet
Setting the upcoming event to "Live" allows people to start checking in. Dee typically send the email to solicit check-in the day before. The app *could* do that automatically if we want it to.
- **The main public site home page** — This is essentially the status quo. It shows a list of *live* league nights, which *should* be a singular event if we're managing them properly. The app won't restrict it to one if we've allowed more than one to remain active. I don't think this is the correct place to default to because it will always require a click to get to something useful; most likely on the live event itself to get to the Current Event page.
- **The Current Event page** — This seems like the logical place to start. From here players can check-in with one tap/click.
- **The event discussion panel** — If a player has already checked-in, and there is at least one post in the discussion, landing here might be a better place. It could drive up excitement about the pending event.
### When an event is live and player cards HAVE been published
After cards have been published there is something more interesting to see than just a check-in
- **The Current Event page** — Landing here would show you who you will be playing with. This is typically the most interesting thing people are waiting for before play begins. It tells them not only who they will share a card with but also which hole they will start on.
- **The scorecard** — This is a more likely page where people will want to focus their attention once play begins. At the moment there is no method to indicate play has begun but it could be assumed to have begun if cards have been published and the start time has passed.
- **The event Leaderboard** — It's also probably that people are less interested in a hole-by-hole view of the current event, as opposed to their overall standing within their division. After all, this is what *really* matters as it relates to the prizes.
### When the event has ended and payouts are set
Once the match ends, people will want to know how they fared
- **The Leaderboard (filtered by Division)** — This is probably the best choice because it tell you if you need to stick around for a prize. There is apparently a problem where people don't know they've earned a prize and they leave. Filtering by division aligns with how the payout are calculated.
- **The Leaderboard (overall)** — This could be a good choice. I know people like to compare their performance against other divisions. I've often heard players in my division say, "I came in 1st in D pool but I would have been 2nd in A pool".
- **The Scorecard** — There *may* be concerns about whether scores were recorded correctly. People might find the scorecard the best place if they think they might have been shortchanged on a hole.
### Then what?
Now that the league night is over, when do we go back to the start of the cycle?
- **The turn of the calendar** — This would keep us on the previous step until midnight, making it easy to look at the event's results at any time after the end until the next day.
- **The next event goes live** — This would keep the results of the completed event until the next event is ready for check-ins
